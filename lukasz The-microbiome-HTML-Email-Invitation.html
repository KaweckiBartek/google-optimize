Dear Colleague,

On behalf of the Global Respiratory Infection Partnership (GRIP). We are delighted to invite you to attend the virtual webinar:

The microbiome: its role in defence and the threat posed by inappropriate antibiotic use

Wednesday 23 March

09.00-09.30 or 18.00-18.30 UTC

SIGN UP NOW

In this webcast, Professor Thomas Bosch will examine the relevance of the microbiome, with a particular focus on the oral microbiome. He will cover the impact of the microbiome on short- and long-term health outcomes, including innate immunity and colonisation resistance. Professor Bosch will also discuss factors that impact the microbiome, with an emphasis on the impact of inappropriate antibiotic use.

Speakers:

Professor Thomas Bosch Kiel University, Germany

Since 2013, Professor Bosch has led the interdisciplinary research centre “Kiel Life Science” (KLS). He is also Principal Investigator and Coordinator of the DFG-funded Collaborative Research Centre “Origin and Function of Metaorganisms”, which addresses the role of multi-organismic interactions for health and disease. Professor Bosch’s lab studies the intricate interactions between the cnidarian Hydra and its colonising microorganisms, and how they affect evolution, development and behaviour.
